# Event Timeline — FOSSHack Jabalpur 2026

This document provides the detailed timeline for FOSSHack Jabalpur 2026. All participants are expected to follow this schedule to remain eligible for evaluation.

---

## Overview

| Date | Event | Format |
|---|---|---|
| March 1, 2026 | Orientation Call | Online |
| March 7, 2026 | Week 1 Update Call | Online |
| March 14, 2026 | Week 2 Update Call | Online |
| March 21, 2026 | Week 3 Update Call | Online |
| March 28, 2026 | In-Person Demo Day | In-Person at GGITS |
| March 31, 2026 | Final Contribution Freeze | — |
| May 4, 2026 | Results Announcement | Online |

---

## Detailed Schedule

### March 1 — Orientation Call (Online)

- Kickoff! Welcome address from the organizing team.
- Introduction to FOSS Hack rules, eligibility criteria, and judging parameters.
- Team formations and introductions.
- Introduction to FOSS licensing requirements.
- GitHub repository setup guidance.
- Q&A session for new participants.

> All registered participants must attend the orientation call. Details will be shared via the registered communication channel.

---

### March 7 — Week 1 Update Call (Online)

- First sync: participants share initial project ideas and repository links.
- Discuss initial progress, architecture, and blockers.
- Early feedback from mentors.
- Guidance on structuring project documentation.
- Discussion of any technical or licensing questions.

> Participants are expected to have their public GitHub repository initialized by this date with a basic README.

---

### March 14 — Week 2 Update Call (Online)

- Mid-point review of active projects.
- Core features should be taking shape.
- Code quality and documentation check-in.
- Mentors provide targeted feedback on individual projects.
- Participants flag blockers or challenges for resolution.

> A minimum of meaningful commits in the repository is expected by this milestone.

---

### March 21 — Week 3 Update Call (Online)

- Final stretch review.
- Focus on bug fixes, polish, and documentation.
- Participants demonstrate early working versions of their projects.
- Feedback on demo presentation and video preparation.
- Reminder of submission requirements and Demo Day logistics.

> All projects should be in a near-complete state by this milestone.

---

### March 28 — In-Person Demo Day

**Venue:** Gyan Ganga Institute of Technology and Sciences (GGITS), Jabalpur, Madhya Pradesh

- Final presentations at GGITS Campus.
- Participants present their projects to the jury panel.
- Demonstration must include a live working version of the project.
- Each team has a maximum of 7 minutes for the demo and Q&A.
- A pre-recorded demo video (maximum 3 minutes) must also be submitted.
- Jury members will evaluate based on the full development history and the Demo Day presentation.

> Physical attendance is required for participants in the Jabalpur local cohort. Remote participants may present via video call if prior approval has been obtained.

---

### March 31 — Final Contribution Freeze

- All commits to project repositories must be completed by 11:59 PM IST on March 31, 2026.
- No commits made after this deadline will be considered during evaluation.
- Final project repository links and submission details must be confirmed before the deadline.
- Make sure your project has an appropriate description and a demo video.
- If you're writing code, make sure to add a link to the source code repository.

> Any commits made after the freeze deadline will be disregarded by the jury.

---

### May 4 — Results Announcement

- After multiple rounds of elimination, results will be shared.
- Projects will be evaluated for novelty, completeness, and relevance.
- Winners will be recognized as part of the national FOSSHack 2026 results.

---

## Important Notes

- Session links and venue details will be communicated to registered participants via the official event channels.
- The organizing team reserves the right to adjust the timeline in exceptional circumstances. Registered participants will be notified of any changes.
- You can participate solo or in a team of up to 4 members.
- Registration is free — [Register here](https://fossunited.org/dashboard/register-for-hackathon?id=1hdcnkbtmk).
