# Event Timeline

FOSSHack Jabalpur 2026 is a month-long, structured open-source hackathon. The timeline below outlines key milestones and sessions throughout the event.

---

## Overview

| Date | Event | Format |
|---|---|---|
| March 1, 2026 | Orientation Session | Online |
| March 7, 2026 | Week 1 Update Call | Online |
| March 14, 2026 | Week 2 Update Call | Online |
| March 21, 2026 | Week 3 Update Call | Online |
| March 28, 2026 | In-Person Demo Day | In-Person — GGITS, Jabalpur |
| March 31, 2026 | Final Contribution Freeze | — |

---

## Detailed Schedule

### March 1 — Orientation Session (Online)

- Welcome address by organizers and FOSS United representatives
- Overview of FOSSHack rules, evaluation criteria, and contribution guidelines
- Introduction to project categories and problem statements
- Q&A session for new participants
- Repository setup guidance

### March 7 — Week 1 Update Call (Online)

- Participants share project ideas and early progress
- Mentors provide feedback on project direction
- Discussion of technical challenges
- Reminder of repository best practices

### March 14 — Week 2 Update Call (Online)

- Mid-event progress check
- Code review and feedback sessions
- Documentation quality review
- Teams encouraged to open issues and pull requests actively

### March 21 — Week 3 Update Call (Online)

- Final feature review before Demo Day
- Participants prepare demo materials
- Feedback on project completeness
- Demo video guidelines reviewed

### March 28 — In-Person Demo Day (GGITS, Jabalpur)

- Venue: Gyan Ganga Institute of Technology and Sciences, Jabalpur
- Participants present their projects to the jury
- Live demonstrations required
- Q&A with jury panel
- Prize distribution ceremony

### March 31 — Final Contribution Freeze

- All commits must be pushed before end of day on March 31, 2026
- No contributions made after this date will be considered for evaluation
- Final project repositories must be publicly accessible

---

## Important Notes

- Attendance at update calls is strongly encouraged but not mandatory
- In-person attendance at Demo Day (March 28) is required for prize eligibility
- All project repositories must remain public throughout the event
- Contributions must be made within the March 1–31 window to count toward evaluation
