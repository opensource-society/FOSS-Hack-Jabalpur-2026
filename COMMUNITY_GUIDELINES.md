# Community Guidelines — FOSSHack Jabalpur 2026

These guidelines govern how participants, contributors, and community members are expected to engage within all spaces associated with FOSSHack Jabalpur 2026. They supplement the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) with practical guidance for day-to-day interactions.

---

## General Conduct

- Communicate respectfully and professionally in all event channels, forums, and repositories.
- Keep discussions relevant to the topic at hand. Off-topic conversations should be moved to designated channels.
- Help build a welcoming environment by being patient with newcomers and participants at different levels of experience.
- Avoid interrupting or speaking over others during online and in-person sessions.
- Acknowledge and credit others' contributions and ideas appropriately.

---

## Online Communication

- Use clear, concise language when asking questions or providing updates.
- Avoid sending duplicate messages or cross-posting the same content to multiple channels.
- Do not share personal contact information of other participants without their explicit consent.
- Refrain from using all-caps text, excessive punctuation, or formatting that may be interpreted as hostile or aggressive.
- Keep announcements and update channels free from general discussion unless specifically instructed otherwise.

---

## GitHub and Repository Etiquette

- Write clear, descriptive commit messages. Follow the standards outlined in [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).
- Do not commit directly to the `main` branch of shared repositories. Always use feature branches and pull requests.
- Before opening a pull request, review your own changes for quality, formatting, and accuracy.
- When reviewing others' pull requests, provide specific, constructive, and respectful feedback.
- Do not close or reject pull requests without providing a reason.
- Report issues using the GitHub issue tracker with sufficient context to reproduce or understand the problem.

---

## Open Source Ethics

- All projects must be genuinely original work created during the event period.
- If your project uses third-party code, libraries, or assets, ensure they are properly licensed and attributed.
- Do not submit work that was created before March 1, 2026 or outside the scope of this event.
- Respect the licenses of open-source software you use. Do not incorporate GPL-licensed code into a project licensed under an incompatible license without understanding the implications.
- Sharing knowledge, tools, and resources with other participants is encouraged, provided it does not violate the spirit of independent contribution.

---

## Collaboration and Teamwork

- Teams may consist of up to four members unless stated otherwise in the event rules.
- All team members are expected to contribute meaningfully to the project. Passive team membership is discouraged.
- Disagreements within a team should be resolved through respectful discussion. If a resolution cannot be reached, contact the organizing team.
- External mentors and advisors may provide guidance, but core development work must be performed by the registered participants.

---

## Inclusivity and Accessibility

- Use inclusive language in all written and spoken communication.
- Avoid assumptions about participants' backgrounds, technical experience, or personal circumstances.
- If you require specific accommodations for accessibility at the in-person Demo Day, contact the organizing team in advance at fosshack@ggits.org.
- Participants from all academic backgrounds and institutions are equally welcome.

---

## Privacy and Data Handling

- Do not collect, store, or process personal data of other participants without explicit consent.
- Projects that involve user data must clearly disclose data collection practices and handle data responsibly in accordance with applicable laws.
- Do not share private communications or data from event channels externally without permission.

---

## Violations

Violations of these community guidelines may result in:

- A private or public warning from the organizing team.
- Removal from event communication channels.
- Disqualification of the participant or team.
- Exclusion from the in-person Demo Day.

Serious violations will be handled in accordance with the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
