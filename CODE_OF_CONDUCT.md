# Code of Conduct

FOSSHack Jabalpur 2026 is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free environment for all participants, mentors, organizers, and contributors.

By participating in this event, you agree to abide by this Code of Conduct.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- Treat all participants with respect and professionalism
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be constructive and helpful in all feedback and discussions
- Accept differing viewpoints and experiences graciously
- Focus on collaboration and shared learning
- Give and receive credit appropriately
- Follow ethical open-source practices at all times

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind
- Offensive, derogatory, or discriminatory comments related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, race, ethnicity, religion, nationality, disability, or age
- Personal attacks or insults directed at other participants
- Trolling, spamming, or deliberately disruptive behavior
- Sharing or threatening to share others' private information without consent
- Submitting work that plagiarizes or misrepresents others' contributions
- Any form of dishonesty, including falsifying project commits or contribution history

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces associated with FOSSHack Jabalpur 2026, including:

- This GitHub repository and all associated pull requests and issues
- Online communication channels (Discord, mailing lists, video calls)
- The in-person Demo Day event at GGITS, Jabalpur
- Any other event-related interactions

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by emailing:

**fosshack@ggits.org**

All reports will be handled with confidentiality and reviewed by the organizing committee. Retaliation against anyone who reports a violation in good faith is not tolerated.

---

## Enforcement

Participants who violate this Code of Conduct may face consequences at the discretion of the organizing committee, including:

- A formal warning
- Removal from the event or repository
- Disqualification from the hackathon
- Reporting to relevant institutions or authorities if required
