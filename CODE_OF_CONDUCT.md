# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

FOSSHack Jabalpur 2026 is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free environment for all participants, contributors, mentors, judges, and organizers, regardless of age, background, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, religion, or any other personal characteristic.

All participants in this event are expected to uphold the standards described in this Code of Conduct.

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

- Conduct themselves professionally at all times, both online and in person.
- Treat every individual with respect and courtesy.
- Provide and receive constructive feedback in a considerate manner.
- Collaborate openly and generously with fellow participants.
- Respect differing opinions, perspectives, and approaches to problem-solving.
- Follow ethical open-source practices, including proper attribution and licensing.
- Comply with the rules and guidelines set by FOSSHack organizers and FOSS United.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

- **Harassment** of any kind, including verbal, written, or physical conduct that creates an intimidating or hostile environment.
- **Discrimination** based on age, background, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, nationality, religion, or any other personal attribute.
- **Spam** — including unsolicited promotional content, repetitive messages, or irrelevant communications in event channels.
- **Plagiarism** — submitting work that is not your own or copying code without proper attribution.
- **Disruptive behavior** that interferes with the event, other participants, or the organizing team.
- **Dishonest conduct** — including misrepresenting project authorship, falsifying commit history, or submitting pre-built work.
- **Intimidation or threats** of any kind, whether directed at participants, judges, or organizers.

---

## Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it immediately by contacting the organizing team:

- **Email:** oss@ggits.org

All reports will be handled with confidentiality and respect. Organizers are obligated to take appropriate action in response to confirmed violations.

---

## Consequences

Participants who violate this Code of Conduct may face consequences including, but not limited to:

- A formal warning.
- Removal from event communication channels.
- Disqualification of the participant's project.
- Removal from the event, including the in-person Demo Day.

The organizing team reserves the right to take any action deemed appropriate to maintain a safe and respectful environment.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces associated with FOSSHack Jabalpur 2026, including:

- GitHub repositories and pull requests
- Online communication channels (Discord, mailing lists, etc.)
- Video calls and online sessions
- The in-person Demo Day at GGITS, Jabalpur

---

*This Code of Conduct is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), version 2.1, and aligns with the [FOSS United Code of Conduct](https://fossunited.org/code-of-conduct).*
